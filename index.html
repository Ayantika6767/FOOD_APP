CAPPUCINO

A cappuccino is an espresso-based coffee drink that originated in Italy and is prepared with steamed milk foam. Variations of the drink involve the use of cream instead of milk, using non-dairy milk substitutes and flavoring with cinnamon or chocolate powder.


EXPRESSO

Espresso is a coffee-brewing method of Italian origin, in which a small amount of nearly boiling water is forced under 9–10 bars of pressure through finely-ground coffee beans. Espresso can be made with a wide variety of coffee beans and roast degrees.


MOCHA

A chocolate-flavoured warm beverage created in the United States,commonly served in a glass rather than a mug. Other commonly used spellings are mochaccino and also mochachino.Like latte, the name is commonly shortened to just mocha.

AMERICANO

Caffè Americano is a type of coffee drink prepared by diluting an espresso with hot water, giving it a similar strength to, but different flavor from, traditionally brewed coffee. Its strength varies with the number of shots of espresso and amount of water added.


CAFFE LATTE

Caffè latte,is a coffee beverage of Italian origin made with espresso and steamed milk.Unlike a hot latte, it does not usually contain steamed milk or foam.Iced lattes often have sugar or flavoring syrups added,they also are served blended with ice.

MACCHATO

Caffè macchiato, sometimes called espresso macchiato, is an espresso coffee drink with a small amount of milk, usually foamed. The intent is that the milk moderates, rather than overwhelms, the taste of the coffee while adding a touch of sweetness.
